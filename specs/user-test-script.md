# User Test Script — The Dead Evening
## Sprint 3 | 3 Tests Required

**Goal:** Validate that real users can complete the concierge flow in < 60 seconds, find at least 1 idea they'd actually do, and understand the save flow without help.

**Format:** 15–20 minutes, in person or screen share. Watch them use it — don't coach. Ask questions after.

**Who to test:** College couples or one person from a couple, age 19–26, Provo/Orem area. NOT your roommates who already know about the app.

---

## Setup (before they arrive)

- Open https://strat490r-product.vercel.app on their phone (not yours)
- Have them sign out if they're signed in: tap Saved → Sign out
- Clear any saved ideas so the session is fresh
- Have a notes doc open to capture observations

---

## Script

### Intro (2 min)

> "I'm going to show you something I'm building and I want to watch you use it. There are no right or wrong answers — if something is confusing, that's important for me to know. I'll watch you go through it and ask some questions after. Talk out loud as much as you can — say what you're thinking while you use it."

**Start the timer** when they land on the home screen.

---

### Task 1 — Core flow (observe silently)

> "Imagine it's tonight, around 8 PM. You're with your girlfriend/boyfriend and you're trying to figure out what to do. Use this to find something."

**What to watch for:**
- Do they immediately understand what to do?
- Do they hesitate on any step?
- Do they read the option labels or just tap randomly?
- How long does the full flow take? (Start → results)
- Do they tap into an idea card?
- What's their facial expression/reaction when they see the results?

**Stop the timer** when they've tapped into at least one idea detail page.

---

### Task 2 — Save flow (observe silently)

> "If you found an idea you liked, what would you do with it?"

(Let them figure out the save button themselves. Don't point it out.)

**What to watch for:**
- Do they find the heart button naturally?
- Do they understand the sign-in flow (magic link)?
- Do they find the saved list afterward?
- Any confusion about "why do I need to sign in?"

---

### Debrief Questions (10 min)

Ask these after both tasks are done. Write down exact quotes.

**About the flow:**
1. "How long did that feel like it took?"
2. "Was there any step where you weren't sure what to do?"
3. "What did you think of the ideas you got? Would you actually do any of them tonight?"
4. "What's missing? What would make this more useful?"

**About the problem:**
5. "How often do you and your partner hit that 'we don't know what to do' moment?"
6. "What do you normally do when that happens?"
7. "Would you use something like this? When?"

**About save/auth:**
8. "What did you think when it asked for your email?"
9. "Did you find where your saved ideas went?"
